## Step 1: Go to a supported platform

Navigate to any supported AI platform in Chrome. For this guide, we'll use ChatGPT, but the flow is identical for Claude, Gemini, and all other supported platforms.

Open a conversation that you'd like to save. It can be any conversation — ongoing or completed.

## Step 2: Click the HAEVN icon

Click the **HAEVN icon** in your Chrome toolbar (the eye/wave logo). A small popup will appear.

On a supported platform, the popup shows:
- The **title** of the current conversation
- Whether it has been synced before (SYNCED / UNSYNCED)
- A **Sync** button to save it now

<Aside type="tip">
  If you don't see the HAEVN icon, click the puzzle-piece icon in Chrome's toolbar and pin HAEVN to make it always visible.
</Aside>

## Step 3: Click Sync

Click the **SYNC** button. HAEVN will capture the full conversation — messages, images, code blocks, and any other content — and save it to your local archive.

The button will briefly show a loading state, then confirm the sync is complete.

## Step 4: Open your archive

Click the **Archive** button in the popup (or right-click the HAEVN icon and choose **Options**).

The archive opens in a new tab, showing all your synced conversations:

<Image src={archiveImg} alt="The HAEVN archive showing a list of synced conversations with platform icons, titles, and timestamps" />

Your newly synced conversation will appear at the top of the list.

## Step 5: Read it in the viewer

Click **Open in Viewer** on any conversation. The viewer opens in a new tab and renders the full conversation with proper formatting:

<Image src={viewerImg} alt="The HAEVN viewer rendering a conversation with formatted messages, code blocks, and metadata" />

The viewer supports markdown, code syntax highlighting, images, thinking blocks, and tool calls — exactly as they appeared in the original platform.
